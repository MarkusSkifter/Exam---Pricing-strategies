\qhead{6. Communicating Value and Prices — How does your company communicate value? What factors are most important in your company's communication of prices?}

BYD's communication is built on \textbf{price-perception psychology}, not just low numbers \parencite{nagle2018}.

\textbf{Reference-price reframing.} ``Electricity cheaper than oil'' shifts the buyer's reference point from \emph{other EVs} to \emph{petrol cars}. Against a Corolla, the Qin looks like both a bargain and an upgrade; BYD picks the comparison that maximises perceived surplus.

\textbf{Gain--loss framing.} The fuel-saving story (\(\sim\)RMB~9{,}682/year) frames the purchase as \emph{avoiding a recurring loss}; because losses loom larger than gains, this beats presenting the same money as a feature ``gain''.

\textbf{Free bundling as anti-anchoring.} Giving ``God's Eye'' assistance \emph{free}, while Tesla charges for Full Self-Driving \parencite{insidechinaADAS}, destroys the rival's price anchor and avoids the Weber--Fechner sticker shock of adding a costly option to a cheap car.

\textbf{Most important factor — the price--quality inference.} In Europe a price ``under half a Tesla'' invites the inference \emph{cheap = poor quality}. BYD counters with \textbf{search-attribute} proof (Blade Battery safety data, warranties) and \textbf{experience-attribute} tools (test drives, showrooms, local Hungarian production), mapping onto the value-communication matrix: lead the economic savings story with hard data, and carry the psychological brand story through storytelling and the halo cars.

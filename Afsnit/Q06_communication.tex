\qhead{6. Communicating Value and Prices — How does your company communicate value? What factors are most important in your company's communication of prices?}

BYD's communication is built on \textbf{price-perception psychology}, not just low numbers \parencite{nagle2018}.

\textbf{Reference-price reframing.} ``Electricity cheaper than oil'' deliberately shifts the buyer's reference point from \emph{other EVs} to \emph{petrol cars}. Against a Corolla, the Qin looks like both a bargain and an upgrade; BYD chooses the comparison that maximises perceived consumer surplus.

\textbf{Gain--loss framing (prospect theory).} The fuel-saving story (\(\sim\)RMB~9{,}682/year) frames the purchase as \emph{avoiding a recurring loss}. Because losses loom larger than equivalent gains, this is more persuasive than presenting the same money as a feature ``gain''.

\textbf{Free bundling as anti-anchoring.} Giving ``God's Eye'' assistance \emph{free}, while Tesla charges thousands for Full Self-Driving \parencite{insidechinaADAS}, destroys the rival's price anchor and signals generosity. It also sidesteps the Weber--Fechner effect: adding a costly paid option to a cheap car would feel proportionally huge, so bundling it in avoids the sticker shock.

\textbf{Most important factor — managing the price--quality inference.} BYD's central communication risk is in Europe, where a price ``under half a Tesla'' invites the inference \emph{cheap = poor quality}. This is the factor that matters most, because mishandling it would convert a price advantage into a brand liability. BYD counters along the value-communication matrix: for \textbf{economic, search} attributes it leads with hard proof (Blade Battery safety data, warranties, awards); for \textbf{economic, experience} attributes it offers test drives and performance evidence; and for \textbf{psychological, experience} attributes it builds the brand through storytelling, the Yangwang halo, and local Hungarian production that signals long-term commitment. The unifying message is that the low price reflects a structural cost advantage, not a quality compromise.

\qhead{2. Making Pricing Decisions, Value and Prices — What are your product's reference and differential values? What are your price floor and price ceiling? Explain your answer.}

For the Qin Plus DM-i I apply the exchange-value framework, \(EV = RV + DV\) \parencite{nagle2018}.

\textbf{Reference value (RV).} The next-best alternative for a mass-market Chinese buyer is not another EV but the \textbf{petrol car} they would otherwise buy — a VW Lavida or Toyota Corolla at roughly RMB~100{,}000--130{,}000. That anchors the reference value.

\textbf{Differentiation value (DV).} \emph{Monetary:} fuel savings of up to about RMB~9{,}682 a year, the new-energy-vehicle tax exemption, and lower maintenance. \emph{Psychological:} modern technology, the free ``God's Eye'' driver assistance, and environmental status. Both are positive, so \(EV\) sits well above the petrol reference.

\textbf{Price floor.} The relevant floor is BYD's \textbf{incremental (variable) cost}, not allocated overhead. Vertical integration and the cell-to-pack Blade Battery — roughly \euro10/kWh cheaper than nickel-cobalt cells \parencite{motleyfool2025} — push this floor unusually low.

\textbf{Price ceiling.} The ceiling is exchange value: the petrol reference price plus monetised differentiation. Above it consumer surplus, \(CS = EV - \text{Price}\), turns negative and the buyer reverts to petrol.

\textbf{The decision.} BYD deliberately prices the Qin \emph{below} the petrol reference (the literal ``electricity cheaper than oil'' claim) while delivering \emph{higher} value, leaving the buyer large consumer surplus and converting ICE demand. It integrates all three pricing inputs — cost (the enabler), competition (priced under petrol rivals) and customer value (the fuel-cost story) — but anchors the price in value, not cost. The decisive nuance is that BYD is \textbf{cost-advantaged but value-led}: cost-plus pricing would have set the price \emph{up} from its low cost and missed the chance to undercut the petrol alternative, exactly the conversion the strategy depends on.

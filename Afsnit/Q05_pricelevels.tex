\qhead{5. Choosing Prices Levels — Which price levels does your company apply? What are the main considerations in that decision?}

BYD's distinctive choice is to run \textbf{different price-level strategies in different segments at once} \parencite{nagle2018}.

\textbf{Penetration in the mass market (Seagull, Dynasty).} Every condition holds: highly \emph{elastic} demand among price-sensitive buyers, large \emph{scale and learning-curve} economies from vertical integration, and explicit \emph{market-share and network} goals (dealer density, charging, brand ubiquity). Crucially, pricing near cost is rational here \emph{because BYD's cost is genuinely below rivals'} — low price does not mean below cost.

\textbf{Skimming at the top (Fang Cheng Bao, Yangwang).} Near-exchange-value pricing — the U9 at about \$3\,million \parencite{electrekU9}. Demand is inelastic and capacity tiny; the purpose is to showcase technology (a halo) and recover R\&D.

\textbf{Neutral-to-skim in Europe.} BYD prices just under Tesla and VW rather than rock-bottom, capturing a tariff-protected margin (Question~12).

\textbf{Defining the window.} The floor is incremental cost (lowered by the Blade Battery); the ceiling is exchange value versus the local reference car. Because the structural cost edge \textbf{widens the window}, BYD has room to choose penetration without dropping below cost — a freedom higher-cost rivals lack.

\textbf{Main considerations and risks.} The choice turns on segment price-sensitivity (elastic mass versus inelastic luxury), capacity and volume goals, and competitive dynamics. Both ends carry risk: penetration ``trains customers to expect low prices'' and can become unsustainable — 2025 net profit fell 19\% despite record volume, and Chinese regulators intervened \parencite{byd2025} — while skimming yields prestige but no scale. The coherent long-run move, visible from 2025--26, is to \textbf{migrate up the ladder} (Denza, Yangwang into Europe), converting a fragile low-price position into a defensible differentiated one before the price war and regulators force the issue.
